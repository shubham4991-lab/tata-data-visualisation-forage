## Overview

This repository contains the work completed as part of the **Tata Group Data Visualisation Virtual Experience** on Forage. The programme simulates a real-world data analyst role, where insights are prepared for C-suite stakeholders (CEO & CMO) of an online retail store.

The dataset covers **December 2010 – December 2011** and includes transactions across **38 countries**, **4,338 unique customers**, and **£10.67M in total revenue**.

---

## Tasks Completed

### Task 1 — Framing the Business Scenario
Reviewed the CEO and CMO's questions and identified which data was relevant. Understood the business context before touching any data.

### Task 2 — Choosing the Right Visuals
Selected appropriate chart types for each question — line chart for time series, bar charts for rankings, and a map visual for global demand — based on what each stakeholder needed to understand.

### Task 3 — Creating Effective Visuals (Power BI)
Built a Power BI dashboard covering four analytical questions:

| # | Question | Stakeholder |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Monthly revenue time series for 2011 | CEO |
| 2 | Top 10 countries by revenue & quantity (excl. UK) | CMO |
| 3 | Top 10 customers by revenue (highest to lowest) | CMO |
| 4 | Global product demand map (excl. UK) | CEO |

**Data cleaning steps applied:**
- Removed returns (negative quantity values)
- Removed erroneous records with unit price below £0

### Task 4 — Communicating Insights and Analysis
Prepared an executive insights report and a video presentation script delivering findings clearly to the CEO and CMO, with strategic recommendations tied to each question.

---

## Key Findings

- **Seasonal spike:** Revenue nearly triples from August → November, peaking at **£1.51M in November 2011**, driven by holiday shopping
- **Top international markets:** Netherlands (£285K), EIRE (£283K), Germany (£229K), France (£210K)
- **Top customer:** Customer ID 14646 generated **£280K** — a classic Pareto concentration in the customer base
- **Expansion opportunity:** Europe dominates demand; **Australia** stands out as the strongest non-European growth market with 84K units sold

---

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Q4 Planning** — Begin inventory build-up and marketing campaigns from August each year
2. **International Focus** — Prioritise Netherlands, EIRE, Germany, and France with localised campaigns
3. **VIP Retention** — Launch a loyalty programme for top 10 customers to reduce churn risk
4. **Expansion** — Establish a mainland Europe logistics partnership first, then expand to Australia
5. **Revenue Diversification** — Run mid-year promotions to reduce over-reliance on Q4 and a handful of customers
